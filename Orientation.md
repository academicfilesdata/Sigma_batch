## 2. Placement & Internship Landscape

### Types of Hiring Routes
| Route | Description |

## 3. Mindset: "Top 10%" Philosophy

- Competition exists everywhere (JEE, NEET, Govt exams) — not just tech.
- Real competition is among the **top 1 lakh**, and real selection happens from the **top 10%**.
- Goal: be in **top 10–20%** of your field — not worry about overall numbers applying.
- College location does **not** matter — everything is online today.

## 10. The Two Pillars: Hard Work & Consistency

| Pillar | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Hard Work** | Minimum 4 hours/day dedicated to prep (lectures + practice included) |
| **Consistency** | Fixed daily schedule — know your 4 hours before you wake up; no "exam-only" study pattern |

> 📌 Exceptions only for: Mid-semester exams (1 hr break before) and End-semester exams (2 hr break before). Resume backlog clearing at 1 lecture/day after exams.

### What NOT to do
- Don't take extra holidays "just because."
- Don't quit on difficult topics (Recursion, Time/Space Complexity) — give them extra time, not skip.
- Don't chase motivation — build a fixed routine instead.

## 11. Notes-Making Strategy

| Chapter Type | Make Notes? |
|---|---|
| Pre-Array topics (variables, data types, loops, patterns, functions) | ❌ No — too basic, will be remembered naturally |
| Array chapters onwards | ✅ Yes |

**What to note:**
- Problems/questions solved in class
- Special properties of data structures (e.g., Binary Tree vs BST vs Trie vs Segment Tree vs Heap — all are trees with different properties)
- Differences (Array vs ArrayList vs Vector vs LinkedList)
- Time & Space Complexity of each solved problem
